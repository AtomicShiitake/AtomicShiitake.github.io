Adsorption (with a d and not a b)

Material flow onto the solid surfaces and are then stuck onto the and become the absorbate. Adsorption can be
split up into two different methods/approaches

Physical

• London Van der walls dispersion, and binds to surface
Variable/Impacts/Aspects
• Heat of Absorption - Small; same as ∆Hvap
• Rate of process - Controlled by resistance to mass transfer; Rapid at low temps
• Specifity - low, Entire surface
• Surface coverage - Complete; Able to multilayer
• Activation Energy - Low • Quantity - High

Chemical

• Chemical Rxn Variable/Impacts/Aspects
• Heat of Absorption - Large; much large than ∆Hvap • Rate of process - Controlled bu resistances on the surface to Rxn • Specifity - High; limited to specific sides • Surface Coverage - Incomplete; Limited to one layer • Activation Energy - High, RxN • Quantity - Low
